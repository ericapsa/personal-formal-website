Timeline and Organizations

Below, in reverse chronological order, are some experiences that I found to be particuarly impactful or taught me a lot. I've also included student organizations important to me.

Important Experiences

Research Work through LocoLab

During the school year, I continued my work at by designing 3D printed exoskeleton attachments for sensors. After iterative feedback loops and a couple redesigns, both attachments are being used on the current exoskeleton. Currently, I'm working on modeling and analyzing biomechanical effects of powered lower-limb orthoses in OpenSim and MATLAB.

Working at the lab as a full-time student has taught me a couple things: 1) How to manage time between school and work commitments, 2) Effective communication with an advisor, and 3) Grit- and following a project from ideation through quality testing.

Presented Exoskeleton Research at SURE Symposium

As part of a 12 week program (Summer Undergraduate Research in Engineering) during the summer of 2021, I worked at LocoLab, an rehabilitative exoskeleton research lab. Here, I helped run human subject experiments to verify if two exoskeleton controllers helped reduce muscle activation during Activities of Daily Living (ADLs). At the end of summer symposium, I presented the research online at the SURE symposium.

Learned and Taught Webpage Code

When the original lockdown summer hit, I turned to online communities to continue learning. From some friends I found Enlight, and completed a two week bootcamp where I created various online projects to learn HTML, CSS, and JS. After graduating from the class, I came back and mentored three other camps after me. This included hosting onine office hours, reviewing projects, and guiding other students to solve coding issues.

Began College at University of Michigan

Enrolled in the College of Engineering in September 2019. Accepted into the College of Engineering Honors Program in March 2021. Go Blue!

Declared a BSE in Mechanical Engineering and Minor in Computer Science.

Graduated from the STEM Academy and FHC

As the pioneer class of the STEM Academy (Science, Technology, Engineering and Math), I enrolled in this 4-year program to take STEM-targeted courses taught in a project-based format that cumulated in a capstone project senior year.

Completed STEM Academy Internship Capstone

As a part of the 4-year program I enrolled in my highschool, my senior year consisted of internship work with companies in the community during the school day. I was the only one to have completed two internships. I presented both my research at GPI and the design process for my prototype in Steelcase during our capstone presentation night!

Presented at the SPCC National Conference

As the culmination of my research internship with GPI Laboratories , my partners and I spent a week in Orlando, Florida, presenting alongside PHD and college graduates students. Although we didn't win the research poster presentation competition, the evaluation committee awarded us with a small monetary prize for being the youngest to compete. Staying ontop of our timeline to finish the research and creating the poster taught me a lot about persistence- to keep moving forward while keeping an eye on the end of the journey.
