About Me

I am an aspiring mechanical engineer, computer science nerd, nature enthusiast, science olympiad board member, violinist and ballroom dancer. I am interested in robotics and sustainable design.

My major was born out of reflecting on my academic habits and experiences in high school. I preferred to methodologically solve problems and trusted mathematical approximations to analyze situations and make decisions. Enrolling in the STEM Academy showed me that engineers solved the problems scientists found and determined my path as an engineer. I chose mechanical engineering through the process of elimination, but ultimately because I wanted a solid general foundation of knowledge to build off of.

I am pursuing a computer science minor because I believe it’s a skill of the future, and that everyone should learn programming regardless of their discipline. I’m passionate about conserving our environment, and determined to make a positive impact on the future and am planning on getting a concentration in the program in sustainable engineering (PISE).

Outside of academics, the Science Olympiad community has been a large part of my life, and I am giving back to the organization by being on board and planning tournaments for middle and high school students. I’m on campus orchestra because violin has been my favorite instrument since I was young, and competitively dance ballroom. Other hobbies include photography and rollerblading.
